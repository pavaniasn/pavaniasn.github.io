Teaching Practice

Philosophy

How I approach teaching

My teaching is built on a single conviction: every learner can succeed when the environment is designed for them, not against them. I came to education through a PhD in life sciences and then through years of teaching first-generation students in government residential colleges — young people for whom university was uncharted territory.

What I found is that rigour and care are not in tension. A well-structured, high-expectation classroom — one that makes the hidden curriculum visible, that explains not just what but why — is where first-generation students thrive.

I bring this same philosophy to faculty development: I teach educators the way I want them to teach their students. Practically. Critically. With room to question.

Methods

What I bring to the room

🎯

Outcomes-Driven Design

Every session is backwards-designed from clear learning objectives. Assessments reveal understanding, not just recall.

🗣️

Active & Participatory

Workshops over lectures. I use case studies, structured discussion, and problem-solving tasks that build transferable skills.

🌱

Equity-Centred

Explicit about what learners need to know, not just what they should already know. Scaffolding that closes gaps rather than assuming them away.

🔬

Evidence-Informed

Draws on learning science — spaced practice, retrieval, metacognition — and adapts based on formative data from participants.
